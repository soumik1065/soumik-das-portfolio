  • Thesis Title: "Development of an Automated Prediction Model of Skin Abnormalities from Photographic Images"
  • Supervisor: Dr. Jhilam Mukherjee, Assistant Professor, Dept. of CSE, SOET, Adamas University, India
  • Description: This project focuses on leveraging deep learning and explainable artificial intelligence (XAI) to improve early detection of skin cancer. Using XceptionNet as the backbone model with transfer learning, the system was trained on a curated dataset of benign and malignant skin lesions sourced from Kaggle, enhanced through data augmentation for robustness. To ensure interpretability, Grad-CAM was integrated to generate heatmaps highlighting the regions influencing the model’s predictions, thus providing clinicians with transparent insights into decision-making. Experimental evaluation demonstrated high accuracy, with XceptionNet outperforming CNN and MobileNetV2, achieving around 91% accuracy with strong sensitivity and specificity. The results validate the model’s potential as a reliable diagnostic aid, offering both precision and interpretability. This work represents a step toward deploying AI-powered tools in dermatology and sets the foundation for a future mobile application that can make skin cancer screening more accessible and user-friendly.
